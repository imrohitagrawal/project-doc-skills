# Usage guide structure — section by section, with examples

Use this order. The examples show the grade-1 voice. Replace the `{{...}}` slots with the project's
real values (from the profile). Keep the licence footer from the profile.

## 1. What this is
One short line.
> **What this is.** This tool helps you check your work. It does the hard parts for you.

## 2. What you need first
A tiny checklist.
> **You need:**
> - A computer.
> - The app, set up.
> - Your sign-in.

## 3. Do this first (quickstart)
The fastest way to a first win.
> **Start here.**
> 1. Open the app.
> 2. Type the start command. Press Enter.
> 3. Wait a moment.
> 4. You will see the word "Ready".
>
> Now you have done it once. 🎉 (Use a ✓ and the word "Done" too, not colour alone.)

## 4. The main things you can do
One short section per task. Each task looks like this:

> ### Make a new check
> **Goal:** make one new check.
> 1. Click **New**.
> 2. Type a name.
> 3. Click **Save**.
>
> **What you will see:** your check shows up in the list, with a ✓.
>
> *(Add a simple picture here. Give it alt text: "The list with one new check and a green tick.")*

Keep tasks small. If a task has many steps, split it into two tasks.

## 5. If something goes wrong
The few common problems. Calm and clear.

> **If you see "Not signed in"** → sign in again, then try once more.
> **If you see a red mark** → check the name has no spaces. Then click Save again.
> **If nothing happens** → close the app and open it again.

## 6. Words we used
A tiny glossary, in kid words.

> **App** — a program you open.
> **Command** — words you type to tell the app what to do.
> **Save** — keep your work so it does not go away.

## 7. Where to get help
Who to ask, and the feedback line.

> **Need help?** Ask {{contact}}, or look at {{docs_home}}. If a step was hard to read, please tell us — we will
> make it simpler.

## Reminders
- Short sentences (about 8 words). One idea each.
- Number every step. Say what they will see.
- A picture for each main task, with alt text.
- Never ask the reader to type a secret here; tell them to sign in or pay the normal way.
- Run the reading-level check and simplify until it passes.
